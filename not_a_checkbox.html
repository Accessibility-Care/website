Accessibility is NOT a checkbox.

Yes, you read that right. In case you haven't seen this profound, utterly original statement splashed across every tech blog, LinkedIn post, and AI-generated "thought leadership" piece, here it is again! Because clearly, repeating it enough times is the same as actually *doing* accessibility.

We get it. It's a journey, not a destination. It's a culture, not a feature. It's a marathon, not a sprint. (Insert any other cliché here for maximum impact). The sheer depth of insight in this phrase is truly breathtaking, especially when juxtaposed with websites that, despite loudly proclaiming this mantra, still manage to be utterly unusable for anyone outside the most privileged demographic.

So, go forth and repeat it. Post it. Share it. Just, for the love of inclusive design, maybe try to remember what it's *supposed* to mean when you're actually building something. Or don't. We're sure the algorithms will generate another perfectly generic, yet deeply insightful, saying for you next week.
